About This Tool

College search shouldn't require insider knowledge. This tool helps you find schools where you can succeed and afford to stay, using transparent data instead of marketing materials.

Why We Built This

The college admissions process often feels designed for families who already know how it works. If you're the first in your family to go to college, or you're relying on Pell Grants and financial aid, you're navigating a system that wasn't built with you in mind.

Most college websites emphasize rankings, campus aesthetics, and sticker prices that few students actually pay. What's harder to find is the information that matters most: What will this actually cost me? Do students like me graduate from here? Will I find support if I struggle?

Our Approach

This recommender focuses on outcomes, not prestige. We prioritize graduation rates, retention rates, and actual net prices—the metrics that tell you whether a school will support your success.

Understanding the Data

Net Price

Most colleges advertise a "sticker price" that includes tuition, fees, room, and board. But almost no one pays that full amount. After grants and scholarships, the net price is what you actually owe. This is the number that affects your daily life—whether you can work fewer hours, buy your textbooks, or focus on studying instead of worrying about bills.

We also calculate an affordability gap, which shows how much you'd need to earn working part-time at minimum wage to cover costs beyond financial aid. It's a reality check: Can I afford this while also succeeding academically?

Graduation and Retention Rates

A school might accept you, but will it support you through graduation? Graduation rates tell you what percentage of students who start at a school actually finish. Retention rates show how many first-year students return for their second year.

We pay special attention to Pell Grant recipient outcomes. If a school has strong graduation rates overall but low rates for Pell students, that's a warning sign. You want a school that supports students like you, not just its wealthiest enrollees.

Minority-Serving Institutions

Some schools are federally designated as Minority-Serving Institutions (MSIs) because they enroll significant numbers of students from underrepresented groups. These include Hispanic-Serving Institutions (HSIs), Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs), and others.

MSIs often provide mentorship networks, culturally responsive support services, and communities where students from similar backgrounds thrive together. Research shows that first-generation students at MSIs graduate at higher rates and report stronger belonging. If community matters to you, these filters can help you find schools built around it.

What Makes This Different

Most college search tools are built by admissions offices or companies with financial incentives. They want you to apply broadly, click on ads, or consider schools that pay for placement.

This tool has no ads, no sponsored results, and no hidden agenda. It's built to help you make the best decision for your situation. We're not trying to maximize applications or drive traffic—we're trying to help students find schools where they'll succeed.

A Note on Match Scores

Your "match score" is personalized to your preferences. It combines your budget limits, desired graduation rates, state preferences, and MSI filters. A 100% match doesn't mean it's the #1 school in the country—it means it's the best fit for what you told us you're looking for.

Who This Is For

This tool is designed with first-generation and Pell-eligible students in mind, but anyone can use it. If you value transparency, affordability, and outcomes over prestige, you're in the right place.

We believe higher education should be a path to opportunity, not an obstacle course designed to favor those who already have advantages. Good data helps level that playing field.
